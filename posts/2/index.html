Tasting of Boulevard / Sierra Nevada - Terra Incognita

First brewed in 2012 for SAVOR attendees, Terra Incognita is a collaboration between the Sierra Nevada and Boulevard breweries which was released commercially in 2013. Terra Incognita is a blend of three different aging processes:

  • Terra Incognita aged in Templeton Rye barrels (30%)
  • Terra Incognita aged in a 2,000-gallon foudre (45%)
  • Fresh Terra Incognita dry-hopped with East Kent Golding (25%)

This blending creates a beer aimed at showcasing some predominant oak characteristics with a subtle hint of earthy hops and which celebrates the unique terroir of the two brewery locations. Then just prior to bottling a dose of the wild yeast Brettanomyces is added and the beer is aged for over three months.

  • Color - 92.5 EBC / 47 SRM
  • Bitterness - 38.5 IBU
  • Original Gravity - 16.9 Plato / 1.069 Specific Gravity
  • Terminal Gravity - 2.10 (after BC) Plato / 1.008 Specific Gravity
  • Alcohol (ABV) - 8.5%
  • CO2 - Bottles - 6 g/L

Tasting of New York Limited - Brooklyn Brewery, Brewery Ommegang, Saranac Brewery

Brewed and bottled at Brooklyn Brewery, Brookly, NY for SAVOR 2013 NYC this beer is a one-of-a-kind collaboration of Brooklyn Brewery, Brewery Ommegang, and Saranac Brewery. With a focus on local New York ingredients, these brewers combined matled barley, raw wheat, yeast, hops, spices, and ideas grown in New York State to bring you a unique take on wheat beer. This white lager, 100% re-fermented in this bottle, is a collaborative nod to the glorious past and brilliant future of New York State brewing. This special release was presented as an exit gift to SAVOR attendees in a 750 ml corked and caged bottle.

  • Special Ingredients:
    • Coriander
    • Orange Peel
    • Lemon Verbena
    • Wildflower Honey
  • Yeast: Lager
  • ABV: 8.5%
  • IBU: 16.5 IBU

Tasting of Samuel Adams - Merry Mischief

This rich dark gingerbread stout entices with the aromas of the holidays. The flavor of gingerbread comes alive, beginning with the smooth sweetness and heartiness of dark roasted malts and a touch of wheat. But it’s the intensity and spices of cinnamon, clove, nutmeg, & ginger that add a wicked kick for a jolly playful brew full of merry mischief.

  • Batch Number: 1
  • Hops:
    • East Kent Goldings

  • Malts:

    • Cinnamon

An Unexpected Encounter

While this actually happened during our Brooklyn Brewery visit, the experience was so incredible it deserved its own post.

I had been wearing my Boulevard Brewery hat all over NYC without a second thought. The Wife and I were sitting at a table in the Brooklyn Brewery tap room enjoying our beer when I noticed the woman across from us looking at me oddly. Full disclosure, I often deserve being looked at oddly but this was not one of those times. Then to my surprise, this happened:

Woman: “Boulevard Brewing, as in Kansas City?”
Me: “Yes. We’re actually here visiting from Kansas City.”
Woman: “My husband and I live in Lawrence, Kansas and are in New York to visit breweries.”
Me: Jaw Drops

In one of the largest cities in the world, two couples who live within 50 miles of one another just happen to sit across from each other at the same table. Now that’s the power of beer.

Brooklyn Brewery - Brooklyn, New York

The first I’d heard about Brooklyn Brewery was from an article in a BYO issue. To be honest, I don’t remember much from the article besides the fact they were an older brewery which I wanted to visit. I should probably go back and read that article. Anyway, since I was already in New York City it was a perfect opportunity for a visit. Let me tell you, I’m glad that I did.

Brooklyn has a distinctly different feel than Manhattan island. When you emerge from the subway, you’re immediately surrounded with by jobs, homes, and real people (not tourists). While I only experienced a few blocks of Brooklyn, it was enough to make an impression.

The brewery’s former life as a factory shows on the outside but the inside is 100% brewery.
Standing guard just inside the main doors are four large fermentation vessels.

Standing in the brewery entrance, to your right you’ll catch a glimpse of the inner workings of the brewery. To your left is the tempting hubbub of the tap room. We went left.

SAVOR: An American Craft Beer & Food Experience

Held in Washington D.C. for the last five years, SAVOR took place this year at the Altman Building and adjoining Metropolitan Pavilion in Manhattan, New York. Attendees enjoy a reception-style sampling of sweet and savoury appetizers and craft beers from over seventy small and independent craft breweries.

If I had to describe my SAVOR experience in one word it would be overwhelming. I say this in the best possible way but I was entirely unprepared for the event. Not that I did not plan ahead (I remembered to wear pants and everything!) and this wasn’t the first beer/wine tasting events I had attended. Maybe it was just the product of a Midwesterner’s first time in the Big Apple but this event was broad in a scope to a degree I had not imagined.

SAVOR offers the opportunity to participate in directed tasting salons featuring stars from the craft beer and food scene. Very few events offer the opportunity to have a one-on-one conversation with the rock stars of the craft beer world. My frantic Ticketmaster antics during the pre-sale managed to secure myself tickets to one of the salon’s:

Apocalypse Now and Again (and Again)

If the world were coming to end, what beer would you brew? In 2012, Elysian Brewing Company answered that question with their 12 Beers of the Apocalypse series. While the world may not have come to an end, the Apocalypse series did, leaving behind a dozen unique beers, each brewed with unusual ingredients. Join Elysian founder Dick Cantwell as he shares samples of four beers from the series and talks about the trials and tribulations of the project.

Only a small increase in the ticket price, the private tasting was money well spent. If the ticket price weren’t so steep I would probably go both days just to have the opportunity to attend two salons. Read on for a look into our SAVOR experience.

Release: BJCP Style Data

Today I am launching the BJCP Style Data repository, a collection of data files containing information from the official BJCP style guidelines. While the BJCP website contains a wealth of beer style information, none of the provided files are conducive to data processing. The goal of this project is to provide the official BJCP style guidelines in multiple data formats which can be easily used in software applications.
