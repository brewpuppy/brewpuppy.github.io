Tasting of Boulevard - Chocolate Ale

A collaboration between Christopher Elbow and Boulevard brewmaster Steven Pauwels, Chocolate Ale harmonizes the chocolate and malt, each supporting and enhancing, but not overwhelming, the other. Elbow recommended a rare variety of chocolate from the Dominican Republic, prized for its robust flavor and aroma. More than a mere flavoring, the crushed, roasted beans were incorporated into the brewing process itself.

Chocolate Ale is full of surprises, beginning with a glowing amber hue. The aroma is given over to earthy, fruity cocoa, with just a hint of hops. Smooth layers of dark chocolate intertwine with threads of caramel, vanilla, and nutty malt as the flavor warms and rounds to a bitter-sweet finish.

  • Hops:
    • Perle
  • Malts:
    • Pale Malt
    • Caracrystal Wheat
    • Simpsons Honey Naked Oats
  • Adjuncts:
    • Dextrose
  • Special Ingredients:
    • Cacao Nibs
  • Color – 41 EBC / 21 SRM
  • Bitterness – 24 IBU
  • Original Gravity – 20 Plato / 1.083 Specific Gravity
  • Terminal Gravity – 4 Plato / 1.016 Specific Gravity
  • Alcohol (ABV) – 9.1%

What I Expected

Speciality Beer (23), base style unknown but my guess is a American Brown Ale (10C).

  • Appearance – Clear, dark amber color. Moderate, light tan head with decent retention from the wheat.
  • Aroma – Fruit notes mixed with a rich cocoa, with just a hint of green/minty hop aroma. The character of the cacao nibs should be evident.
  • Flavor – High caramel, vanilla, and nutty malt flavors. Medium bitter finish from the chocolate. Slightly sweet from the caramel malts. Medium body finishing with some alcohol warmth. The distinctive flavor character associated from the cacao nibs should be noticeable.

Tasting Notes

  • Appearance – Copper color with medium-high clarity. The head is an ivory with low retention, possibly from being on draft. The CO2 bubbles rising from the sides of the glass from the higher level makes the beer quite the looker.
  • Aroma – Exactly how a chocolate ale should smell. The cocoa is the dominant aroma with sweet caramel malt coming in a distant second. Possibly some fruit mixed in there but the cocoa has beaten the other aromas into submission.
  • Flavor – Heavy malt, with grainy, caramel, and cocoa dominating. Reminds me of the flavors of a Tootsie Roll but with a rich vanilla backbone. The cacao gives the beer a different sort of bitterness which doesn’t linger.
  • Mouthfeel – Dry finish which evaporates off the tongue. Light bodied and effervescent with lingering alcohol warmth.

Conclusion: Excellent (43 / 50)

Guest Tasters:

  • The Wife: Outstanding (47 / 50)

Appearance: 2 / 3

The practically non-existent head cost the beer one point.

  • Second Opinion – The Wife: 2 / 3
    • Head: Low Size and Retention, Cream Color
    • Medium Clarity
    • Amber/Brown Color

Aroma: 8 / 12

I did not experience any earthy or hop aromas which were not overpowered by the cacao. There was perhaps a touch of fruitiness, but I could have been making that up in my head it was so slight. While I quite enjoyed the aroma, I had to dock the beer for not being as described.

  • The Wife: 11 / 12
    • High Sweetness
    • Caramel malt
    • Chocolate Cherries

Flavor: 19 / 20

I was sceptical of how the dominating cocoa would work out, but the taste is almost exactly what I expected.

  • The Wife: 19 / 20
    • Medium-High Caramel Malt
    • Medium-High Sweetness, Medium-Low Bitterness
    • Medium Earthy Hops
    • Even Balance
    • Berry and Chocolate flavors (chocolate covered cherries)
    • Just enough bitterness to cut the sweetness at the end

Mouthfeel: 5 / 5

The light body prevents the beer from feeling too heavy from the sweetness and chocolate flavors.

  • The Wife: 5 / 5
    • Medium-High Body
    • Medium Carbonation
    • Low-Medium Warmth and Creaminess
    • Dry Finish
    • Smooth with slight tingle on the back end with the hop flavor

Overall Impression: 9 / 10

Chocolate is usually associated with sweet, which this beer is not. As someone who likes drier beers, this beer makes for the perfect dessert pour. I really want to try making ice cream with this beer.

  • The Wife: 10 / 10
    • I would pay money for this beer; please send me the recipe!
    • Yes please, chocolate dessert.
