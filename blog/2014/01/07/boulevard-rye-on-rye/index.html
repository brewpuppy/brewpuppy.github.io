Tasting of Boulevard - Rye-On-Rye

Rye is an assertively flavorful grain more often associated with whiskey than with ales but has largely been eclipsed by corn and barley, the sources of bourbon and scotch. When Boulevard procured seasoned barrels from Templeton Rye, they chose to brew a rich, tawny rye ale, then mellowed it in the warmth of charred oak rye whiskey casks. Two kinds of malted rye provide spicy sweetness, giving way to notes of caramelized wood and the citrusy tang of Citra and Ahtanum hops before easing into a dry, lingering finish.

Heads up! Despite what your bottle label may say, Boulevard Brewer Jeremy Danner has confirmed the hop schedule listed here is correct.
  • Hops:
    • Magnum – Bittering
    • Citra – Bittering/Flavor
    • Styrian Golding – Flavor/Aroma (in kettle)
    • Ahtanum – Flavor/Aroma (in whirlpool)
  • Malts:
    • Pale Malt
    • Malted Rye
    • Munich
    • Cara 50
    • Crystal Rye
  • Adjuncts:
    • Dextrose
    • Brown Sugar
  • Color – 44 EBC / 22 SRM
  • Bitterness – 47 IBU
  • Original Gravity – 22.5 Plato / 1.094 Specific Gravity
  • Terminal Gravity – 2.8 Plato / 1.011 Specific Gravity
  • Alcohol (ABV) – 12%
  • CO2 – Bottles – 6.8 g/L

What I Expected

What first springs to mind for me is an imperial version of an American Rye Beer (6D) but the color is far too dark to be true to the style, not to mention the oak aging. The extra bitterness should balance out the rich malts as long as the spice of the rye is not overpowering.

  • Appearance – Dark amber more in line with a porter or stout and a nice solid head from the rye.
  • Aroma – Oak, caramels, and maybe a touch of citrus or spicy notes.
  • Flavor – Sweet caramels from the crystal malts and spiciness from the rye on the front. Warmth from the alcohol. Rich sweetness from the high OG balanced with the bitterness of the hops and rye. I’m not sure how the citrus flavors of the hops will fit in but I’m hoping they compliment the spiciness of the rye. Oak flavors always take me by surprise in their individual character so while I’m expecting the oak flavors to be noticeable, I’m not sure exactly how it will come through.

Tasting Notes

  • Appearance – Beautiful amber, mostly clear with just a touch of turbidity. Average dark beige head which dissipates quickly.
  • Aroma – Caramel and dark fruit notes with some rye spiciness pulling everything together. Brett sourness comes to mind, possibly from the combination of hops and barrel character.
  • Flavor – Simple elegant flavors of rich caramel and a sourness/citrus of hops which I found extremely similar to Brett. Rye was mellow but in a good way. Pleasant warmth of alcohol complements the sweet bourbon twang from the barrels.

Conclusion: Excellent (44 / 50)

Guest Tasters:

  • The Wife: Excellent (44 / 50)

Appearance: 2 / 3

The low head retention cost the beer one point, but was otherwise spot on.

  • Second Opinion – The Wife: 3 / 3
    • Head: Smooth Texture, Medium Size, Medium-Low Retention, Cream Color
    • Low Clarity
    • Amber/Brown Color

Aroma: 10 / 12

The caramels and rye spiciness were present, but the sour notes while pleasant, were definitely unexpected.

  • The Wife: 10 / 12
    • High levels of Rich, Caramel, Roasty malt
    • Medium-Low Hops
    • Medium-High Sweetness
    • Slight Brett Smell

Flavor: 19 / 20

The complimentary flavors from the malts and the oak tie everything together. Even the citrus sourness which I was so sceptical about fit perfectly into the overall experience.

  • The Wife: 18 / 20
    • Medium-High Hops
    • Citrusy Hops similar to the tang of Brett
    • Medium-High Rich, Caramel Malt
    • Even Balance
    • Twang of brett or citrus hops
    • Sweet tingle at the front which mellows with a slight bittering of oak flavor at the end

Mouthfeel: 4 / 5

A bit more carbonation than I would prefer in this style; rough on the mouth. However, I let mine breathe for a few minutes and by then it was perfect.

  • The Wife: 4 / 5
    • Sweet Finish
    • Nice elevated amount of carbonation
    • Sweet beginning with smooth finish leaves mouth clean
    • Citrus tingling flavor once the drink is done

Overall Impression: 9 / 10

For a “harsher” rye beer, this is quite drinkable. The balance is spot on for what I enjoy drinking. The oak flavors, especially the bourbon, are great as the beer gets closer to 50F. However, I do not recommend drinking the beer about 55F because the balance goes totally off the wall.

  • The Wife: 9 / 10
    • I would pay money for this beer; please send me the recipe!
