Pumpkin Beer Showdown: Round 4

It’s an Autumn miracle! Just when I had given up hope, these latecomers had shown up to join the showdown. Even after tasting over a dozen different pumpkin beers I’m still excited to see how this batch turns out. Which brewery’s late-to-the-party beverage will emerge from Round 4 victorious? Let’s find out.

Rogue Farms – Pumpkin Patch Ale

The newest addition to the Chatoe Rogue series is Pumpkin Patch Ale, a beer made using fresh pumpkins grown in a patch that borders Rogue’s 42 acre hop yard. The pumpkins are picked, loaded into Rogue’s farm truck, driven immediately 77 miles to our brewery in Newport, quickly roasted, and pitched into the brew kettle to create a batch of Pumpkin Patch Ale.

  • 14 PLATO
  • 25 Lovibond
  • 75 AA
  • IBU: 25
  • Hops: Rogue Micro Hopyard Rebel Hops
  • Malts: Great Western 2-Row, Carawheat, Weyermann Carafe Malts
  • Yeast: Pacman Yeast
  • Special Ingredients: Rogue Farms Pumpkins, Ginger, Cloves, Vanilla bean, Cinnamon, Cardamom, Nutmeg

Brew Puppy: 6 / 10

Mild nutmeg aroma. Complex flavors of allspice and clove make this beer difficult to decipher but in a good way. However, there is a bit too much astringency on the back-end (maybe the orange peel?) which overstays its welcome. Unfortunately, the extra bitterness at the end kept this from being one of my favorites.

The Wife: 6 / 10

Sweet pumpkin nose and a soft spice smell. Smooth malt front flowing easily into spice bitterness. Slight lingering flavor of cinnamon and cloves.

Mother’s – Mr. Pumpkin

Our first release from Mother’s Cupboard, Mr. Pumpkin is a medium bodied, copper color ale. With just the right blend of 2 Row, Caramel, and Crystal malts, pumpkin and spices, Mother’s has created one yummy pint of love. Perfect after dinner in lieu of dessert, or for a night out on the town. Enjoy out doors on cool fall nights, or when slaving over the stove making that holiday dinner for the in-laws. If pie can be for breakfast, well it certainly should work as beer.

  • ABV: 5.0%
  • IBU: 10
  • Color: Copper colored medium to medium-light bodied
  • Malts: Pale ale, Crystal 35
  • Hops: Very little hops
  • Special Ingredients: touch of Roast pumpkin before addition for some carmelization and pie spice in kettle and post fermentation

Brew Puppy: 6 / 10

Good spice nose of ginger and allspice. Dry finish and pleasant amount of allspice/cinnamon/nutmeg which balances with the malts. Not quite enough body in this beer which lost it points for me.

The Wife: 6 / 10

Cinnamon and allspice nose. Lingering cinnamon front-end flavor with mild to modest front-end bitterness and only a small malt flavor.

Woodchuck – Pumpkin Cider

Every once in a while you know you stumble upon something glorious. That something just so happens to be our Private Reserve Pumpkin. We have combined our signature taste with a refreshing pumpkin finish. Limited to just two and half hours on the production line this is a true connoisseur’s cider.

Brew Puppy: 2 / 10

Aroma is an icepick of sweetness straight into the sinuses. The combination of cider and spices tastes like cheap flavoured gum. Couldn’t bring myself to take more than one drink so that’s all the analysis I’ll be providing.

The Wife: 6 / 10

Strong apple cider nose. Beautiful dark color. Bubble gum sweet, candy apple flavor with no bitterness. Not sure where the pumpkin flavor fits in.

Results

  1. 6
    Rogue Farms – Pumpkin Patch Ale
  2. 6
    Mother’s – Mr. Pumpkin
  3. 4
    Woodchuck – Pumpkin Cider
