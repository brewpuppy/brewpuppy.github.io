Tasting of Boulevard / Sierra Nevada - Terra Incognita

First brewed in 2012 for SAVOR attendees, Terra Incognita is a collaboration between the Sierra Nevada and Boulevard breweries which was released commercially in 2013. Terra Incognita is a blend of three different aging processes:

  • Terra Incognita aged in Templeton Rye barrels (30%)
  • Terra Incognita aged in a 2,000-gallon foudre (45%)
  • Fresh Terra Incognita dry-hopped with East Kent Golding (25%)

This blending creates a beer aimed at showcasing some predominant oak characteristics with a subtle hint of earthy hops and which celebrates the unique terroir of the two brewery locations. Then just prior to bottling a dose of the wild yeast Brettanomyces is added and the beer is aged for over three months.

  • Color – 92.5 EBC / 47 SRM
  • Bitterness – 38.5 IBU
  • Original Gravity – 16.9 Plato / 1.069 Specific Gravity
  • Terminal Gravity – 2.10 (after BC) Plato / 1.008 Specific Gravity
  • Alcohol (ABV) – 8.5%
  • CO2 – Bottles – 6 g/L

What I Expected

I did not have much to work with so this is nothing but guesswork. The vital statistics match that of a stout (category 13) with the ABV making my best approximation as an imperial stout.

  • Appearance – Dark in color, almost black with a rich head to match.
  • Aroma – Mild earthy hop flavors mixed with some oak notes from the barrel aging.
  • Flavor – Earthy and spicy flavors from the hops with an rich oak back-end balanced by the warmth of the alcohol. Because the Brettanomyces is added late in the process I don’t expect it to be a dominant flavor but rather something subtle in the background.

Tasting Notes

  • Appearance – Huge tan colored head which was rock-solid and seemed to last forever. Beer was deep copper with good clarity.
  • Aroma – Strong earthy hop aroma with a touch of rye spiciness and lactic sourness.
  • Flavor – The rye and oak flavors blend well on the front of the taste. Some sweetness balanced with a dry bittering on the finish.
  • Mouthfeel – Medium carbonation despite the huge head (which annoying kept getting on my nose). Dry finish.

Conclusion: Very Good (33 / 50)

Guest Tasters:

  • The Wife: Excellent (37 / 50)

Appearance: 3 / 3

The color and head of this beer was exactly what I expected which deserves a perfect score.

  • The Wife: 2 / 3
    • Head: High Size, High Retention, Frothy Texture, Tan Color
    • Brown/Black color
    • Lace
    • Too much head

Aroma: 10 / 12

While the hops flavors were present in the aroma I was missing the oak notes.

  • The Wife: 10 / 12
    • Caramel, Roasty, Medium-High Malt
    • Earthy, Medium Hops
    • Low-Medium Sweetness
    • Bittersweet oak background smell

Flavor: 10 / 20

Why is the hop gone? Strangely, despite the bold hop aroma little to none of the earthy hop flavors which dominated the aroma remain.

  • The Wife: 17 / 20
    • Rich, Toasty, Roasty, Medium Malt
    • Earthy, Spicy, Medium Hops
    • Low Sweetness
    • Low Acidic Back-end
    • Even Balance

Mouthfeel: 4 / 5

Unfortunately, the Hulk Hogan head got in the way of drinking this beer.

  • The Wife: 5 / 5
    • Medium-High Body
    • Medium Carbonation
    • Medium Warmth
    • Low Creaminess
    • Medium, Dry Finish

Overall Impression: 6 / 10

This beer would be nice to have once in a while but not on a regular basis. I would be more willing to drink one on a crisp Autumn day as opposed to a hot Summer afternoon. Even then I would probably only drink one glass.

  • The Wife: 3 / 10
    • High Stylistic Accuracy
    • High Technical Merit
    • Low Intangibles
    • I would finish this sample
