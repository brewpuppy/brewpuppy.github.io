Tasting of Samuel Adams - Tasman Red

This wily red IPA gets its character from the Tasmanian hops that are full of grapefruit, pine, and earthy notes creating a bold flavor that threads throughout the taste. The hops are balanced by a core of roasty malts that give this brew body and richness with hints of toffee. This flavorful brew is rounded and smooth with a dry and citrusy hop finish.

  • Batch Number: 1
  • Hops:
    • Topaz
    • Galaxy
  • Malts:
    • Samuel Adams two-row pale malt blend
    • Special B
    • Caramel 60
    • Carafa III
  • Yeast: Top-fermenting Ale
  • Color: Rich garnet, 45 SRM
  • ABV: 6.5%
  • IBUs: 50

Brewer’s Tasting Notes:

  • While no grapefruit was used in this beer, the use of Topaz and Galaxy hops give this beer a bold hop character taste/aroma that can be compared to a grapefruit.
  • No caramel was used in this beer, though roasty caramel notes can be picked up through a blend of different malts.

What I Expected

Jim Koch has a great tasting video which I used to base my expectations. It’s only a few minutes long but offers great insight into this particular beer.

A big malt front to it and a big hop finish.

Jim Koch, Tasting Video Tasman Red

The use of IPA in the name immediately puts this in the style India Pale Ale. An ABV under 7.5% rules out 14C Imperial IPA. The special malts used in this beer push the SRM far above either the English IPA (14A) or American IPA (14B) styles. With all the malts in this beer it doesn’t look like it will fit under any of the classic styles. If I had to pick one to judge it on though, it would be American IPA for the Aroma and Flavor described. I’m a big fan of hops from Down Under (oh Nelson Sauvin, I want to go there) so I’m excited these are the highlight of the beer.

  • Appearance – Deep ruby red which really come across as a beer which takes malt seriously; the color should reflect this idea. Malt flavors are not joking matter folks.
  • Aroma – I’m looking for the malts to make an appearance and not be overpowered by the hops. Specific smells I’d like to see (smell?) are caramel and toffee as well as grapefruit and pine from the hops.
  • Flavor – The flavor should be exactly what I smelled. sweet caramel toffee with some roasted notes balanced by lots of grapefruit and citrus along with spruce and pine for a resiny finish.

Tasting Notes

  • Appearance – Deep red, a bit hazy. Pours beautifully with a full creamy head.
  • Aroma – Definitely picking up on the citrus and pine of the hops but the malt was lost to me until the beer set for a few minutes, and only then did caramel flavors dominated the aroma.
  • Flavor – Where the aroma failed, the taste certainly delivered. Roasty and caramel malt flavors which transitioned to the resinous hops which then transitioned into a grapefruit bitterness on the end. After the first few drinks when my palate was used to the bitterness the malt flavors really started to come and took a front seat.

Conclusion: Excellent (40 / 45)

Appearance: 2 / 3

The color was exactly what I expected though the lack of clarity was a bit surprising coming from a professional such as Samuel Adams.

Aroma: 10 / 12

At first I was a bit disappointed with the aroma being hop-centric and out of the gate this beer smells like any other American IPA. However, when I smelled again after sitting a while, the malts were there for my enjoyment.

Flavor: 19 / 20

Two words: flavor delivers. This beer lives up to the promise of big malt front and a big hop finish; exactly what I expected. The caramel and resin flavors blend wonderfully. The lower IBU makes it very drinkable as only the malts linger on the palate. I would recommend this beer drank at a warmer temperature to fully appreciate the malt flavors. Maybe not as much grapefruit as what I was expecting so I docked it one point.

Overall Impression: 9 / 10

A complex beer which felt like a three-stage rocket to drink. This is something I would definitely buy again when I want a one-off IPA. I would recommend this beer to IPA drinkers who are looking for something different. I would not recommend this beer to Red Ale drinkers unless you’re feeling particularly bold. While there are pleasant malt flavors the hops dominate this beer as any true IPA should and is nothing similar to the styles you may be used to drinking.
