Brooklyn Brewery - Brooklyn, New York

The first I’d heard about Brooklyn Brewery was from an article in a BYO issue. To be honest, I don’t remember much from the article besides the fact they were an older brewery which I wanted to visit. I should probably go back and read that article. Anyway, since I was already in New York City it was a perfect opportunity for a visit. Let me tell you, I’m glad that I did.

Brooklyn has a distinctly different feel than Manhattan island. When you emerge from the subway, you’re immediately surrounded with by jobs, homes, and real people (not tourists). While I only experienced a few blocks of Brooklyn, it was enough to make an impression.

The brewery’s former life as a factory shows on the outside but the inside is 100% brewery.
Standing guard just inside the main doors are four large fermentation vessels.

Standing in the brewery entrance, to your right you’ll catch a glimpse of the inner workings of the brewery. To your left is the tempting hubbub of the tap room. We went left.

Address

Brooklyn Brewery
79 N 11th Street
Brooklyn, New York 11249
www.brooklynbrewery.com

AHA Perks

  • 10% off Merchandise & Cases
  • 1 Free Beer during Happy Hour & Tours (Friday & Saturday)

Tap Room

The tap room was the large generic room you would expect to see from a warehouse. While the room was filled with wooden tables and benches, finding an open seat was a challenge; the demand for this brewery has definitely outgrown its supply. Whenever a your ends, the line to the tokens to purchase tokens becomes a ten minute wait and the line for the beer becomes a fifteen to twenty minute wait. I would recommend grabbing two beers per person when you finally reach the bartenders. Speaking of bartenders, they all wear the Brooklyn Brewery shirts available for sale which is an excellent way to sneak a preview before you buy. Of course, I could not resist picking up another shirt for my collection. Even though the wait was long the beer was worth it. The ones we tried were a bit hoppy for The Wife which means they were right up my alley. The price of beer (five tokens for $20) is worth mentioning. One token will get you most beers with a few speciality beer costing two tokens. This is a killer price for any bar, let alone one in New York City which explains why the place was packed even on a Sunday.

Tour

The free tour starts in the main entryway with nowhere near enough seats for the large crowd gathered; no sign-up or reservations is required. We non-intentionally placed ourselves at the front of the tour, but unfortunately the tour consisted of a single room so it didn’t make much of a difference. While the information given by the presenter was interesting and well delivered, I felt a bit disappointed at not seeing more of the brewery. Granted the room was quite large and packed with enough equipment to glimpse all aspects of the brewing process, it still felt like just a glimpse. That said, I do not regret going on the tour one bit or even a byte (hahaha, computer pun!) and I definitely recommend you do it at least once. Be sure to bring a beer with you on the tour though, as you’ll be standing in one place for a while.

Conclusion

The drink prices alone make the Brooklyn Brewery difficult to pass up; throw in the fact that the beer is good and it becomes a required stop. While there was music always playing it was not overly loud or obnoxious as you would find in most bars or clubs. I envy the group who snagged a large table early and brought a board game (Settlers of Catan I believe) for the evening. Others brought in pizza which is the encouraged food of choice for the tap room. Unfortunately I did not have a chance to try all the beers which means the I will definitely be returning to visit the Brooklyn Brewery the next time I’m in New York City. Maybe I’ll see you there next time.

Under no circumstances should you visit this brewery if you are the future savior of mankind from intelligent killer robots.
Unfortunately, I did not witness any sword-fighting. Maybe next time?
